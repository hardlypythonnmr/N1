**Layer 1: Meta-Theory (Always Active)**
One entry per framework: Name (Author, Year) | Key finding | How N1 uses it

Entries to include:

1. Protean Career Theory (Hall, 2004) — Careers are self-directed and values-driven, measured by psychological success not salary/title. N1 premise: "el sistema eres tu."
2. Career Chaos Theory (Pryor & Bright, 2011) — Careers are complex nonlinear systems; prediction is limited; building adaptability > planning. N1: normalizes uncertainty, emphasizes CAAS.
3. Planned Happenstance (Krumboltz, 2009) — Unplanned events are inevitable and desirable; 5 skills (curiosity, persistence, flexibility, optimism, risk-taking) help capitalize on them. N1: encourages experiments over rigid plans.
4. Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan, 1985) — Three universal needs: autonomy, competence, relatedness. SDT-based career chatbot outperformed generic chatbot (CHI 2025). N1: career satisfaction check against three needs.

**Layer 2: Integrative Spine** 5. SCCT — Social Cognitive Career Theory (Lent, Brown & Hackett, 1994) — Self-efficacy → outcome expectations → interests → goals → actions. The causal engine connecting busca adentro to construye afuera. N1: every recommendation traces this chain. 6. Schlossberg 4S (Schlossberg, 1995) — Transitions require: Situation + Self + Support + Strategies. N1 triage layer maps to Situation; busca adentro = Self; construye afuera = Strategies.

**Layer 3: Busca Adentro — Values Dimension** 7. ACT Values Clarification (Hayes et al., 1999) — Psychological flexibility reduces career rigidity; committed action toward values outperforms goal-setting alone; g = 0.57-0.82 (large effects, meta-analysis 2024). N1: primary values framework. 8. Schwartz Basic Values Theory (Schwartz, 1992) — 10 universal values organized in motivational continuum; values hierarchy predicts career choices across cultures. N1: values profiling in busca adentro. 9. SDT Self-Direction (Deci & Ryan, 1985) — Autonomy (self-direction) + competence + relatedness as universal career needs; 144 studies, 79K+ participants. N1: values-career fit check.

**Layer 4: Busca Adentro — Strengths Dimension** 10. Bandura Self-Efficacy (Bandura, 1997) — Four sources: mastery experiences, vicarious learning, social persuasion, physiological states. Strongest career performance predictor. N1: self-efficacy mapping in strengths dimension. 11. VIA Character Strengths (Peterson & Seligman, 2004) — 24 universal strengths organized in 6 virtues; Gold Standard baseline (with Big Five). viacharacter.org (free, validated). N1: signature strengths identification; refer users there. 12. Flow Theory (Csikszentmihalyi, 1990) — Optimal experience occurs when challenge matches skill; flow activities signal career fit. Strong evidence. N1: Flow Activity Logger. 13. Big Five Personality (Costa & McCrae, 1992) — Gold Standard personality model; IPIP-NEO validated free version. Openness predicts creative career success; Conscientiousness predicts performance across domains. N1: bigfive-web embed (MIT license).

**Layer 5: Busca Adentro — Identity Dimension** 14. Working Identity (Ibarra, 2003) — Career transitions require experimenting with new identities before committing; "act your way into a new way of thinking" outperforms introspection alone. Moderate evidence. N1: crafting experiments in identity dimension. 15. Identity Status Theory (Marcia, 1966) — 4 statuses: achieved (committed + explored), moratorium (exploring), foreclosed (committed without exploring), diffused (neither). N1: heuristic assessment in identity dimension; entry-level moratorium is PRODUCTIVE. 16. CAAS — Career Adapt-Abilities Scale (Savickas & Porfeli, 2012) — 4 subscales: Concern, Control, Curiosity, Confidence; 149 studies, 82,519 participants; strongest career-specific predictor; no consumer tool exists. N1: CAAS-12 static web tool + identity dimension. 17. PsyCap HERO (Luthans et al., 2007) — Hope + Efficacy + Resilience + Optimism; predicts performance and well-being across career stages. N1: enrichment layer; used in Go Independent path.

**Layer 6: Busca Adentro — Purpose Dimension** 18. MLQ — Meaning in Life Questionnaire (Steger et al., 2006) — 10 items measuring Presence (having meaning) and Search (seeking meaning); MLQ-10 most validated meaning instrument. High Search normative in LATAM. N1: MLQ-10 static web tool + purpose dimension. 19. Logotherapy (Frankl, 1946/1984) — Meaning through creative work, experiential values, and attitudinal values (suffering with dignity); purpose reduces mortality by 17% (Hill et al., 2014; 136,265 participants). N1: meaning sources framework in purpose dimension. 20. Ryff Psychological Well-Being (Ryff, 1989) — 6 dimensions: autonomy, environmental mastery, personal growth, positive relations, purpose, self-acceptance. Strong evidence. N1: well-being lens for career recommendations. 21. Life Design (Burnett & Evans, 2016) — Odyssey Plans (3 parallel 5-year paths), reframing work/play/love/health. Most engaging exercises. Moderate evidence. N1: purpose dimension exercises.

**Layer 7: Career Strategy Frameworks** 22. Granovetter Weak Ties (Granovetter, 1973) — Weak ties (acquaintances) provide novel information; most jobs found through weak ties, not close contacts. N1: Network Map tool. 23. AAQ-II — Acceptance and Action Questionnaire (Hayes et al., 2004) — 7-item psychological flexibility measure; score 7-49; higher = more flexible; predicts career decision quality. N1: enrichment layer. 24. CDMSE-SF — Career Decision-Making Self-Efficacy (Betz & Taylor, 1994) — 25-item self-efficacy for career decisions; predicts career exploration and commitment. N1: enrichment layer.

**Layer 8: Prompting Techniques** 25. MIND-SAFE (JMIR, 2025) — Crisis detection and safety protocol for AI mental health tools; Tier-based escalation reduces harm. N1: safety-protocol.md. 26. Chain of Empathy (arXiv:2311.04915) — Reasoning about user emotional state before responding improves response quality and safety. N1: CoE in prompting-stack.md. 27. SELF-DISCOVER (Hugging Face/DeepMind, NeurIPS 2024) — Dynamic reasoning module selection outperforms fixed prompting; 32% improvement. N1: technique selection in prompting-stack.md. 28. Motivational Interviewing AI (arXiv:2505.17380, JMIR 2025) — MI techniques in AI counseling reduce resistance and improve engagement. N1: OARS in prompting-stack.md.
