# STAR Drilling — Structured behavioral question practice

> Reference loaded by `skills/win-interviews/SKILL.md`.
> STAR (Situation-Task-Action-Result) is the most validated framework for behavioral interview structure. Drilling it reduces rambling, improves recall, and raises offer rates.

---

## What STAR is (and isn't)

STAR is:
- **A narrative scaffold** for answering behavioral questions ("Tell me about a time...")
- **A memory aid** — easier to recall a 4-part structure than a long story
- **An interviewer-friendly format** — interviewers score against rubrics that map cleanly to STAR

STAR is NOT:
- A script to memorize verbatim (sounds robotic)
- The only valid answer structure (PAR, CAR, and others are variations)
- Necessary for every question (tell-me-about-yourself uses a different arc)

---

## The four parts

| Part | What | Typical length | Common mistake |
|------|------|----------------|----------------|
| **Situation** | Context the listener needs | 10-15s | Over-setting the scene; giving too much background |
| **Task** | Your specific responsibility | 8-12s | Skipped; interviewer doesn't know what was *yours* to do |
| **Action** | What YOU did | 20-30s | Saying "we" when it was you; missing specifics |
| **Result** | Outcome, ideally measured | 10-15s | Vague ("it went well") or missing entirely |

Total: 60-90 seconds for most behavioral answers.

## Drilling methodology

### Drill 1 — Structure without content

Pick 5 behavioral questions. For each, answer in 60 seconds with **explicitly labeled parts**:

> "Situation: We had to migrate... 
> Task: I was responsible for... 
> Action: I did three things... 
> Result: We ended up..."

This feels robotic on purpose. Goal: embed the structure until automatic. 3-5 rounds of this, then drop the labels.

### Drill 2 — Hook-first opening

Strong interview answers start with a **hook** before S. One sentence that tells the interviewer what's coming:

- "Había un momento en Rappi donde había que migrar a 12 analistas a una plataforma nueva."
- "Cuando entré a Globant como senior, mi nuevo manager me dio feedback que me costó procesar."

Drill: for each of your 5-6 bank stories, write and rehearse the hook. 10 seconds max.

### Drill 3 — Compress and expand

For one story, practice telling it in:
- **30 seconds:** hook + 2-sentence S/T combined + 2-3 key actions + result
- **60 seconds:** full STAR, standard format
- **2 minutes:** full STAR with additional context + learning at the end

Goal: adaptive storytelling. Some interviewers want short, some invite detail. You match.

### Drill 4 — The mid-story recovery

Interviewers interrupt. "Wait, before you continue, what was your team's size?" or "Why that approach instead of X?".

Drill: tell a story; have a partner (or AI) interrupt 2-3 times with tangent questions. Practice answering the tangent cleanly, then returning: "Volviendo a lo de la migración, lo que pasó después fue..."

### Drill 5 — Pattern-matching new questions

Interviewer asks an unfamiliar question. You have one of your 5-6 stories that fits.

Question patterns and their likely story matches:

| Question pattern | Probable story from bank |
|------------------|--------------------------|
| "Time you led change" | Leadership story |
| "Time you had conflict" | Conflict story |
| "Time you disagreed with manager" | Conflict or Ambiguity |
| "Time you made a mistake" | Failure story |
| "Biggest project you shipped" | Impact story |
| "Something you learned fast" | Growth story |
| "Time you mentored" | Leadership (secondary frame) or Growth |
| "Time you improved a process" | Impact (secondary frame) |
| "Time you worked under pressure" | Multiple options; pick the strongest |

Drill: have partner (or AI) generate 15-20 questions at random. For each, name which story from your bank you'd use **within 3 seconds**. If you can't, expand your bank or re-frame an existing story.

## Answer openers (hook library)

Strong:
- "Había un momento donde..."
- "Cuando entré a [role / company], la situación era..."
- "Me asignaron [task] y rápidamente vi que..."
- "Mi equipo de X estaba [state], y yo tenía que..."
- "Recibí feedback de [source] que..."

Weak:
- "Una vez..."
- "En general yo hago..."
- "Siempre he sido de los que..."
- "Déjame pensar... creo que podría contarte..."
- "Hmm, buena pregunta..."

Strong hooks anchor in specifics. Weak hooks stall.

## The Result problem

Most candidates under-deliver the Result. Patterns:

### Missing Result entirely
Candidate describes Situation, Task, Action in detail, then... silence. Interviewer has to prompt: "And what happened?"

**Fix:** every story ends with R stated explicitly. Even if metric is small, state it: "Shippeamos en 3 semanas, ahorramos 15 horas/semana de trabajo manual, el equipo adoptó sin capacitación adicional."

### Vague Result
"It went well." "The team liked it." "We were happy with the outcome."

**Fix:** force one number. If no metric exists, force an observable:
- "La tasa de adopción fue del 92% en 2 semanas" (metric)
- "Ningún miembro del equipo volvió a mencionar el proceso viejo en el siguiente sprint" (observable)
- "Mi manager referenció el proyecto en la next performance review como ejemplo" (observable)

### Result without ownership
"The company grew 40% that year." Were you the reason? Unclear.

**Fix:** make your contribution explicit. "El proyecto que lideré contribuyó X puntos del growth de 40%. El resto vino de [other factors]."

### Learning — the optional 6th part

Some answer structures add a 5th L (Learning) or 6th element (What you'd do differently). Use this when:
- The Result is ambiguous or mixed → "What I took from this was..."
- The interviewer asked "what did you learn" or "what would you do differently" explicitly
- You want to transition from a success story to self-awareness

Don't force Learning if the story already landed. Over-reflection sounds like therapy.

## Common drilling failure modes

- **Over-memorization:** candidate repeats same exact sentences every time. Interviewer senses rehearsal, drops trust.
  - Fix: tell each story 5x in different words. Internalize structure, not script.
- **Under-preparation:** candidate winged 5 behavioral questions and realizes in the interview they repeat the same story in different disguises.
  - Fix: build the bank of 5-6 stories BEFORE the interview.
- **Wrong-story match:** candidate forces a story that doesn't quite fit the question.
  - Fix: in drill mode, say "this question matches story X because..." — practice the matching itself.
- **Abandoning STAR mid-story:** candidate starts strong, meanders, forgets Result.
  - Fix: silent "STAR checkpoint" — after Action, pause 1 second, check: "did I give Situation? Task? Did I say what I did specifically? Do I have a Result?"

## Drilling frequency

Based on Yoodli / Final Round AI / Interview Sidekick aggregate data:

- **15-30 min daily for 2 weeks** before an important interview = ~60% improvement in performance metrics (filler words, pacing, completeness)
- **Weekly 45-min mock + self-recording** = sustained improvement over months
- **Pre-interview cram (3 hours the night before)** = anxiety high, performance mixed, fatigue on interview day
- **Optimal cadence:** short daily + weekly mock session >> long infrequent

## Checklist before interview day

- [ ] 5-6 stories indexed by competency
- [ ] Hook rehearsed for each story (< 15 seconds)
- [ ] Each story told in 60-second version at least 3 times
- [ ] One story told in 30-second and 2-minute variants
- [ ] Filler word count measured and reduced to < 8 per 60-second answer
- [ ] WPM measured and in 130-160 range
- [ ] Closing (next-steps) language rehearsed
- [ ] Questions for interviewer prepared (3-4 distinct angles)
- [ ] Night-before ritual: 30 min review only, then sleep
