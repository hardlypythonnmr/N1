# Role Calibration — Tuning interview prep by context

> Reference loaded by `skills/win-interviews/SKILL.md`.
> The same story told to a startup CTO and a bank hiring manager lands differently. Calibrate register, pacing, and emphasis per context.

---

## Axis 1 — Company stage

| Stage | Expected register | What they want to see |
|-------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Pre-seed / Seed (1-15 people)** | Casual, fast, direct | Scrappiness, comfort with ambiguity, willingness to do multiple roles |
| **Series A-B (15-75)** | Warm, intentional, forward-looking | Systems thinking, ability to level-up as company grows, ownership mindset |
| **Series C+ / late stage (75-500)** | Professional but not corporate | Specialization + ability to scale practices, political navigation |
| **Public / enterprise (500+)** | Formal, structured | Domain expertise, process fluency, stakeholder management |

**Calibration moves:**
- Pre-seed: shorten stories, use "I" liberally, emphasize scrappy bias-to-action
- Enterprise: longer stories with more context, emphasize stakeholder navigation, name teams and processes

---

## Axis 2 — Role orientation

| Role type | What they weight |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Individual contributor (IC) technical** | Depth of execution, specific decisions, why one technical choice over another |
| **IC business** | Outcomes with metrics, stakeholder management, judgment under uncertainty |
| **Engineering Manager / Team Lead** | People decisions, hard feedback moments, balancing individual and team outcomes |
| **Senior / Staff IC** | Scope, influence without authority, pattern recognition across projects |
| **Client-facing / Consulting** | Translation between technical and non-technical audiences, handling pushback, building trust |
| **Founder / Entrepreneurial** | Resourcefulness, bias-to-action, managing uncertainty, resilience |

**Calibration moves:**
- IC technical: go deeper on technical decisions; interviewer wants to hear your reasoning
- Manager: proportion of stories should shift — fewer "I built X" stories, more "I navigated Y people situation"
- Senior/Staff: emphasize influence + pattern thinking; the story is about the SYSTEMS you changed, not tasks you shipped

---

## Axis 3 — Interview type

| Type | Prep focus |
|------|-----------|
| **Screening (recruiter)** | Clarity of story, fit-check answers, salary expectations, timeline |
| **Hiring manager 1:1** | Deep behavioral, domain knowledge, role-specific scenarios |
| **Technical deep-dive** | Specific work, code review / case study, tradeoff discussions |
| **Panel / cross-functional** | Multiple simultaneous priorities, working well with varied stakeholders |
| **Final / executive** | Vision, judgment under high stakes, strategic thinking |
| **Behavioral full-round (Amazon-style)** | All 5-6 competency stories, drilled and ready |
| **Case / simulation** | Thinking-out-loud, hypothesis framing, clarifying questions first |

**Calibration moves:**
- Screening: keep answers short (45-60s), over-explain fit, clarify logistics
- Technical deep-dive: expect 10+ minute discussions on one topic; don't front-load
- Panel: read the room, vary eye contact, don't favor one questioner
- Case: the first 30 seconds should be YOUR clarifying questions, not jumping to solution

---

## Axis 4 — Industry vertical

| Industry | Register hints |
|----------|----------------|
| **Fintech / banking** | Formal, regulatory awareness, risk literacy, skepticism of hype |
| **Tech / SaaS** | Modern, metric-fluent, comfortable with fast iteration |
| **E-commerce / marketplaces** | Unit economics fluency, operational rigor |
| **Healthtech** | Serious, patient-centered framing, regulatory respect |
| **Consulting / agencies** | Client-first framing, translation skills, structured thinking |
| **Non-profit / social impact** | Mission-aligned language, humility, impact measurement |
| **Public sector / government** | Formal register, process respect, stakeholder orientation |

**Calibration moves:**
- Fintech: never frame a story as "I moved fast and broke things" — they'll see you as a risk
- Agency / consulting: emphasize translation and adaptability; stories about "working with client X" land well
- Non-profit: lower the outcome metrics, raise the mission alignment — "we served 3K people" beats "we grew revenue 30%"

## Axis 5 — Geography & culture

Colombia / LATAM-specific:
- Warmth in tone is expected, even in formal contexts
- Voseo for Colombian interviewers is neutral; tú can feel stiff for Medellín/Bogotá hiring managers
- Avoid Argentine voseo lexicon ("che", "pibe", "laburo") with Colombian interviewers
- "Usted" appropriate only if interviewer uses it first
- References to Rappi, Mercado Libre, Nubank, Globant land cleanly as context
- International remote (US-based with LATAM team): code-switch to English cleanly; don't mix

Spain:
- Tú is standard; voseo sounds regional
- Slightly more formal register than LATAM

US (if LATAM candidate interviewing in English):
- Be aware of translation habits (literal translations that sound odd in English)
- "At my prior company" beats "in my last employment"
- Stories about LATAM work need brief context ("Rappi, the super-app serving 180M users in LATAM")

## Decision tree — how to calibrate a specific interview

Given: company, role, interview type, interviewer.

1. **Look up company stage** (Crunchbase / LinkedIn / website "about")
2. **Identify role orientation** (IC/manager, technical/business, senior/junior)
3. **Confirm interview type** (recruiter screen / hiring manager / technical / panel)
4. **Check industry vertical**
5. **Check interviewer background** (LinkedIn: how long at company, prior roles, posts they write)

Apply calibration moves from each axis. Some will conflict — when they do, weight in this order:
- Role orientation (highest)
- Interview type
- Company stage
- Industry
- Geography

## Sample calibrated prep

**Target:** Senior Product Manager at a Series B fintech in Bogotá, interviewing with the Head of Product (who was previously at Nubank).

- **Stage calibration:** Series B = balance ownership mindset + scaling. Not pre-seed scrappy, not enterprise-formal.
- **Role calibration:** senior PM = expect influence-without-authority stories, prioritization decisions, stakeholder alignment
- **Interview type:** hiring manager 1:1 = expect deep behavioral
- **Industry:** fintech = regulatory awareness, risk framing, skepticism of "move fast"
- **Geography:** Colombia = warm voseo register
- **Interviewer background:** ex-Nubank = probably cares about product craft + user obsession (Nubank culture)

**Prep implications:**
- Lead stories with outcomes measured in financial terms (revenue, retention, unit econ)
- Emphasize at least one story where you slowed down for risk/compliance (fintech will test for this)
- Use warm voseo but stay professional (Head of Product is senior)
- Frame stakeholder stories around user outcomes (Nubank influence)
- Prepare 2-3 clarifying questions about the role that show you understand product craft, not just strategy

---

## When calibration conflicts

Some interview contexts pull opposite directions. Examples:

- **Series B fintech** pulls toward "scrappy + ownership" AND "regulatory rigor" — you need stories that show BOTH
- **Senior IC at enterprise** pulls toward "formal process" AND "influence without authority" — hybrid mode
- **Consulting to startup** — prep for client-fluency (your strength) but also demonstrate you can sustain focus (startups worry consultants are fickle)

When contexts conflict, ask during interview prep: "What does this specific team need that's harder to find?" Lead with THAT.
