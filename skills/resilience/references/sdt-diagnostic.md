# SDT Diagnostic — Autonomy, Competence, Relatedness for job search

> Reference loaded by `skills/resilience/SKILL.md`.
> Self-Determination Theory (Ryan & Deci 2000) identifies three basic psychological needs whose satisfaction predicts intrinsic motivation and whose frustration predicts burnout. Adapted here to the job-search context.

---

## Why SDT for job search

Job search is a sustained behavior with little external reward until late (when offer arrives). Most people start with some motivation and slowly lose it. SDT names why:

- **Autonomy** need: feeling your choices are yours. Job search starts feeling imposed ("I must apply").
- **Competence** need: effort yields visible progress. Applications vanish into the void → competence frustrated.
- **Relatedness** need: feeling connected. Job search is often done alone, in isolation, with shame.

When all three needs are satisfied, persistence is high. When any is frustrated, dropout rate climbs. Senécal et al. 2025 (BMC Psychology): job-search persistence correlates 0.4+ with combined SDT need satisfaction.

## The three dimensions — deeper

### Autonomy (autonomía)

Not freedom from constraints. Autonomy = acting from your own values and interest, vs. external pressure or internal compulsion.

**Autonomous motivation in job search:**
- "I'm applying to roles I genuinely want because the work matches what I care about."
- Choices are made with awareness and acceptance.

**Controlled motivation (autonomy frustrated):**
- "I have to apply because I'll lose runway / face / identity." (introjected)
- "My family expects me to have a job by X." (external)
- "I apply to whatever shows up." (amotivation masked as action)

Signs of autonomy loss during prolonged search:
- Apply volume increases, quality decreases (spray and pray)
- Roles chosen to "just get something" vs. roles chosen for fit
- User speaks of search in passive terms ("they keep rejecting me") vs. active ("I'm choosing where to apply")
- Growing resentment toward the process itself

### Competence (competencia)

Feeling effective and capable. Competence is not skill; it's the *experience* of effective action.

**Competence satisfied:**
- Actions produce visible results (applications get responses, including rejections — data is better than silence)
- Feedback loops work (CV changes → interview rate changes)
- User can see their own progress (more interviews this month than last)

**Competence frustrated:**
- Effort without feedback (500 applications, 3 responses, 0 interviews)
- Mechanics feel broken (ATS rejection with no reason; recruiters ghost)
- User can't tell if effort is working

Signs of competence loss:
- Apathetic rationalizations ("the system is broken" — may be true, but leads to inaction)
- Learned helplessness pattern ("nothing works, why bother")
- Declining effort quality (CVs not tailored, applications rushed)

### Relatedness (pertenencia)

Feeling connected to others who care.

**Relatedness satisfied:**
- At least one person knows the user is searching AND asks how it's going
- Community of some kind (peers, professional group, mentor)
- User feels seen as a person, not just a CV

**Relatedness frustrated:**
- User hides the search from everyone (shame, pride, protecting family)
- No one asks how the search is going; user can't initiate the conversation
- Community interactions are transactional (networking events feel like performance)

Signs of relatedness loss:
- Withdrawal from social connection
- Shame spirals ("I shouldn't be complaining," "others have it worse")
- Loneliness + rumination loop

## The diagnostic protocol (3 questions)

In the Resilience skill, ask these three — one per dimension:

```
1. AUTONOMÍA
   ¿Las últimas 5-10 aplicaciones fueron a roles que vos elegiste
   activamente, o fueron "lo que aparecía" / "cualquier cosa que me
   saquen de acá"?

2. COMPETENCIA
   ¿Tus esfuerzos están generando feedback visible (respuestas,
   rechazos con razón, entrevistas, avances) o es un vacío negro?

3. PERTENENCIA
   ¿Hay alguien acompañándote en esto — un amigo, pareja, mentor,
   grupo? ¿O estás haciendo esto solo/a?
```

Score each informally: satisfied / mixed / frustrated.

## Pattern interpretation

### All three frustrated
The user is in genuine burnout territory. Intervention is not "try harder" — it's recovery first.
- Evaluate MIND-SAFE Tier 2
- Recommend: deliberate pause (5-7 days of no applications), then restart with one dimension at a time
- Do NOT pile on more tactics

### Autonomy frustrated, C/R okay
Return to N1 Prepare — clarify role-archetype, reconnect with values. Reduce application volume, increase intention per application.

### Competence frustrated, A/R okay
Return to N1 Get Interviews — audit the CV/LinkedIn. The feedback loop is broken; fix that before more volume.

### Relatedness frustrated, A/C okay
One-action intervention: tell ONE person this week. Not "build a network" (that's overwhelming). One specific person, one conversation. Often this single move unblocks momentum.

### Autonomy + Competence frustrated
Usually means applying to roles that are simultaneously not-aligned AND not-responsive. Return to Prepare + Get Interviews; the search is fundamentally mis-aimed.

### Autonomy + Relatedness frustrated
Often: the user is applying to roles that feel imposed (family / financial) in isolation. Pattern of suffering performance. Intervention: name the pressure explicitly, consider what's chosen vs. forced, identify one person who can hold context.

### Competence + Relatedness frustrated
Feedback-less isolation. Classic pre-burnout. Intervention: one external support (peer, mentor) + one feedback loop fix (CV audit, interview rehearsal with a person who gives real feedback).

## Intervention principles

### Restore autonomy
- "You don't have to apply this week. You have to DECIDE what you want to do about the search. Those are different."
- Remove false urgency. Most searches are not actually as time-sensitive as they feel.
- Reconnect with criterion: "Which 3 roles would make this whole process worth it?"

### Restore competence
- Fix one feedback loop (CV audit that changes interview rate; or get to one mock interview that changes confidence)
- Scale down to one visible win per week ("1 response this week" not "land a job")
- Name progress when it appears (even small)

### Restore relatedness
- One conversation, not a network
- Person who can witness the search, not fix it
- "Tell [name] that you're looking. Ask them to check in weekly." That's it.

## Cultural calibration (LATAM)

- **Collectivist context:** relatedness in LATAM often satisfied through family, but **rarely about the job search specifically**. Family may know you're unemployed but not follow the process detail. The user might feel surrounded and still isolated in the search.
- **Pride culture:** shame around unemployment is high in professional contexts. "Decir que estás desempleado" has weight. Honor this — don't push users into public disclosure.
- **Working-class solidarity:** some users have strong community support (especially in smaller cities) that N1 should not undervalue. Don't assume isolation.

---

## What NOT to do

- **Don't prescribe therapy** unless Tier 2 signals present. SDT frustration is common in job search, not diagnostic of disorder.
- **Don't moralize.** "You should take care of yourself more" is condescending. Name the pattern, offer an intervention, respect choice.
- **Don't catastrophize.** Most users recover with small interventions. Resilience is restorable.
- **Don't confuse SDT with motivation coaching.** The goal is not to "get motivated." It's to identify where a specific need is frustrated and address that specifically.
