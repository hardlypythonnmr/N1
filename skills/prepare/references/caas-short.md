# CAAS-Short — Conversational career adaptability snapshot

> Reference loaded by `skills/prepare/SKILL.md`.
> **Important:** This is a 4-item conversational approximation. The validated instrument is CAAS-12 (Savickas & Porfeli 2012). Full version available at `web/compass/`.

---

## What CAAS measures

Career Adapt-Abilities Scale measures four **psychosocial resources** for adapting to career tasks, transitions, and traumas. CAAS-12 is the short form (12 items, 4 per dimension), 82,519 participants across 149 studies.

### The four dimensions (C's)

| Dimension | What it measures | Behavioral signal |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Concern** | Orientation toward the future; planning mindset | Thinks ahead, imagines future scenarios |
| **Control** | Sense of personal responsibility for career | Makes decisions, owns outcomes |
| **Curiosity** | Exploring possible selves and career options | Investigates, considers alternatives |
| **Confidence** | Self-efficacy to pursue career aspirations | Acts on plans, persists through obstacles |

---

## The short-form protocol (4 items)

One item per dimension. Ask in order. Each scored 1-5.

```
1. ¿Qué tanto pensás en tu futuro profesional a 3-5 años?
   Del 1 (casi nunca) al 5 (constantemente).
   [CONCERN]

2. ¿Qué tanto sentís que tus acciones definen tu carrera (más que circunstancias externas)?
   Del 1 (casi nada) al 5 (en gran medida).
   [CONTROL]

3. ¿Qué tan frecuentemente explorás opciones profesionales que nunca habías considerado?
   Del 1 (casi nunca) al 5 (frecuentemente).
   [CURIOSITY]

4. ¿Qué tanto confiás en que podés lograr las metas profesionales que te pongas?
   Del 1 (poco) al 5 (mucho).
   [CONFIDENCE]
```

## Interpretation patterns

### Profile 1 — "Planned and Active" (4+, 4+, 3+, 4+)

High Concern + Control + Confidence. The person is actively planning and owning their career.

- **Strength:** agency and focus
- **Risk:** may be rigid, over-invested in one path, under-exploring
- **Intervention:** boost Curiosity (structured exploration of adjacent paths)

### Profile 2 — "Exploring and Open" (3-4, 2-3, 4+, 3)

High Curiosity + moderate Concern. Exploration mode but not yet committing.

- **Strength:** open, gathering information
- **Risk:** indecision paralysis; moratorium extending too long
- **Intervention:** use Ibarra's Working Identity — small committed experiments, not permanent decisions

### Profile 3 — "Concerned but Stuck" (4+, 2, 2-3, 2-3)

High Concern (worries about future) + low Control + low Confidence.

- **Pattern:** anxiety without agency
- **Risk:** rumination without action; learned helplessness
- **Intervention:** behavioral activation (micro-goals, implementation intentions) — see N1 Resilience

### Profile 4 — "Confident but Narrow" (2-3, 4+, 2, 4+)

High Control + Confidence + low Concern + low Curiosity. Good operator in current role; not thinking ahead or exploring.

- **Risk:** blindsided by industry change or role obsolescence
- **Intervention:** adaptability education — future scenarios, weak-tie informational conversations

### Profile 5 — "Disengaged" (2, 2, 2, 2)

Low across all. Either:
- Severe burnout / depression — evaluate MIND-SAFE Tier 2
- Genuine "I don't care about career right now" (valid stance — could be health, family, sabbatical phase) — honor it, don't force engagement

## Cultural calibration (critical for LATAM)

From Porfeli & Savickas (2012) cross-cultural validation: CAAS scores differ systematically by culture.

**LATAM norms (Colombia specifically):**
- **Control** runs ~0.5 SD lower than US norms. This is collectivist culture artifact — decisions framed as consensus, family-influenced, community-embedded. NOT learned helplessness.
- **Concern** similar to global norms.
- **Curiosity** slightly higher in LATAM (culturally normative to explore).
- **Confidence** similar, with regional variation.

**Do not pathologize lower Control in LATAM users.** Frame: "Tu Control está en 2. En contexto colombiano, eso es común y no indica pasividad — simplemente reflejá un estilo más colectivo de toma de decisiones. La pregunta es: ¿estás viendo posibilidades que querés activar, o te sentís bloqueado?"

## Bridging to skill outputs

Given the CAAS profile, the skill calibrates downstream:

- **Low Control →** Resilience skill recommendation (behavioral activation)
- **Low Curiosity + high Concern →** Get Interviews with skill-to-title translation (surface paths they haven't seen)
- **Low Confidence →** Win Interviews with emphasis on story-based confidence building (Bandura mastery experiences)
- **Low Concern →** prepare to plan more explicitly (3/6/12-month roadmap)
