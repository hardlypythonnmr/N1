## What MLQ measures

Meaning in Life Questionnaire (Steger 2006) measures two independent dimensions:

- **Presence:** the degree to which one experiences life as meaningful right now
- **Search:** the degree to which one is actively seeking meaning

These are independent — a person can be high or low on each, producing four quadrants.

---

## The short-form (2 items)

```
1. ¿Qué tanto sentís que tu trabajo tiene sentido para vos hoy?
   Del 1 (nada) al 7 (mucho).
   [MLQ-PRESENCE]

2. ¿Qué tan activamente buscás sentido o propósito en lo que hacés?
   Del 1 (nada) al 7 (mucho).
   [MLQ-SEARCH]
```

---

## The four quadrants

| Presence | Search | Quadrant | Pattern |
|----------|--------|----------|---------|
| High (5-7) | Low (1-3) | **FULFILLED** (realizada) | Current work has meaning, not actively looking. Meaning is settled. |
| High (5-7) | High (5-7) | **GROWING** (creciendo) | Has meaning + wants more. Healthy expansion. |
| Low (1-3) | High (5-7) | **SEEKING** (buscando) | No current meaning, actively searching. Productive transition phase. |
| Low (1-3) | Low (1-3) | **DISENGAGED** (desconectada) | No meaning + no search. Warning signal. |

---

## Quadrant implications for career work

### FULFILLED
The user's job-search issue is not meaning — it's something else (compensation, geography, growth, team). Don't solve a meaning problem they don't have. Address the actual pain.

### GROWING
Healthy. They want to deepen meaning without losing what they have. Job search should prioritize roles that expand meaning rather than replace it. Look for: more impact, more autonomy, more craft in current domain.

### SEEKING
Most common in people actively transitioning. They're in a productive exploratory phase. Don't rush them to certainty. Support the exploration with structure (Ibarra's Working Identity — small experiments). Do NOT interpret high Search as pathological.

### DISENGAGED
Warning signal. Consider:
- Burnout (if user reports exhaustion, depersonalization, reduced efficacy) — refer to Tier 2 resources
- Depression (if user reports persistent low mood, anhedonia, hopelessness) — MIND-SAFE Tier 2 referral
- Genuine "I don't care about meaning right now; I want stability" — valid, honor it
- Life transition (new baby, grief, health) — meaning lives outside work right now

## Cultural calibration (critical)

From Steger 2009 cross-cultural validation:

**LATAM + Colombian norms:**
- **High Search scores are culturally normative.** Active search for meaning is not pathological — it's a cultural pattern of continuous reflection.
- **Do NOT tell Colombian users "you should have more Presence, less Search."** That's pathologizing a normal cultural response.
- **US/Northern European users often score lower on Search.** When comparing to US "norms," adjust.

**From N1 voice-guide.md §10:** "High MLQ-Search (Colombia/LATAM): Actively searching for meaning is normative in LATAM populations. Do not interpret high MLQ-Search scores as symptomatic of existential disorder."

## Integration with workflow

Given the quadrant, calibrate the session:

### FULFILLED quadrant
- Skip further meaning work. Focus on instrumental career tasks.
- User's question is likely operational: how to get interviews / win interviews / negotiate

### GROWING quadrant
- Surface which specific dimensions of meaning the person wants MORE of:
  - Creative (Frankl): what they give to the world
  - Experiential (Frankl): what they receive (relationships, beauty, growth)
  - Attitudinal (Frankl): stance toward unavoidable difficulty
- Use role-archetype to target roles that amplify these

### SEEKING quadrant
- Longer exploratory session warranted
- Use Ibarra's Working Identity framework:
  - Possible selves: list 3-5 professional identities worth exploring
  - Active experiments: commit to ONE small experiment (a project, a conversation, a course) to test the fit
  - Re-evaluate in 2-4 weeks
- Do not push decision. Seeking is valid; premature commitment kills exploration

### DISENGAGED quadrant
- Evaluate MIND-SAFE signals
- If no Tier 1/2 signals: honor the user's stance ("I don't want meaning, I want a job that pays and leaves me alone at 6pm") — it's valid
- Pivot to Get Interviews or Resilience depending on presentation
- Do NOT moralize about meaning

## When to skip MLQ altogether

- User explicitly says they don't want to talk about meaning / purpose
- Session is focused on urgent tactical need (have interview tomorrow, negotiating offer this week)
- User has MLQ data in their N1 YAML profile from < 6 weeks ago — use that instead of re-measuring
