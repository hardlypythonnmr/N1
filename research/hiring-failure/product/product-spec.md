## 1. Product Vision

N1 is the first Spanish-language career strategy system that treats career change as what the science says it is: an identity transition that requires psychology, structured systems, and execution velocity to navigate successfully. Every existing career product solves one fragment — a resume tool here, an interview course there, a motivational book somewhere else — while ignoring that 80% of professionals feel unprepared for the 2026 job market (LinkedIn, n=19,000) and that the median online course completion rate is 12.6% (MOOC research). N1 integrates the full arc from "I need to change" through "I've successfully transitioned and I'm thriving" into a single subscription-based system built on peer-reviewed science, delivered through community accountability, and designed for LATAM professionals in Spanish. No product like this exists. Not in English. Not in Spanish. Not anywhere.

### 2.2 Tools (Free)

#### N1 Diagnostic Lite
- 15-question self-assessment (subset of the full 42-question Compass Calibration)

- Built in: Notion form or Typeform, report delivered via email automation

#### CV Quick-Check (Checklist)
- 12-item research-backed checklist based on the highest-impact interventions from Stage 1 research:
  1. Is it tailored to this specific role? (40-50% more callbacks — ResumeGo)
  2. Does it include 5+ quantified achievements? (2.3x more likely to interview — Cultivated Culture, n=125K)
  3. Is the word count 475-600? (8.2% interview rate, nearly 2x average)
  4. Does it use tenure format over dates for gaps? (+15% callbacks — Kristal et al., Nature Human Behaviour, n=9,022)
  5. Are keywords at 60-80% match with the job description?
  6. Is there a professional email address? (30% rejection for unprofessional — CareerBuilder)
  7. Is the length appropriate for experience level? (2-page for 10+ years = 2.3x more callbacks — ResumeGo, n=7,712)
  8. Are there zero typos/grammar errors? (80% rejection trigger — hiring manager surveys)
  9. Is the format ATS-parseable? (no tables, text boxes, or headers/footers)
  10. Does it include a tailored cover letter? (50% more interviews — ResumeGo; 35.8% vs 21.2% offer rate)
  11. Is there a complete LinkedIn profile? (40x more opportunities — LinkedIn)
  12. Are the first 3 bullet points under each role the strongest? (7.4-second scan prioritizes top of each section — TheLadders)
- Free downloadable PDF, shareable

#### Phone Screen Prep Checklist
- 7-item preparation protocol based on Stage 2 research:
  1. Read the job description line by line and map each requirement to a specific experience
  2. Research the company beyond the homepage (recent news, strategy, product)
  3. Prepare a 60-second "tell me about yourself" connecting background to this specific role
  4. Prepare 3-5 questions that demonstrate strategic thinking (not FAQ-answerable)
  5. Practice speaking aloud — phone screens penalize unclear communication disproportionately
  6. Express explicit interest: "I'm very interested because..." (recruiters report this is shockingly rare)
  7. Prepare salary range with market data but deflect to later stages if possible

#### STAGE 0: Pre-Search — Identity, Strategy, Positioning

**The problem this solves:** 80% of professionals feel unprepared for the 2026 job market. Most career products assume you know what you want. Stage 0 addresses the people who don't — the "employed but unhappy," the recently laid off, the ones in pre-transition where psychology is everything.

**Content modules:**

**0.1 — Identity Diagnosis**
- The Identity Stack framework: Heredada (inherited identity from family, education, early career) → Construida (identity built through work experiences) → Elegida (the identity you choose going forward)
- Based on: Career change as identity transition (Annual Reviews of Organizational Psychology, 2024)
- Exercises: Identity mapping, values excavation, the "who am I without my title?" prompt
- Psychological framework: Grief processing in career loss (job loss follows the same psychological pattern as other major losses)

**0.2 — Self-Efficacy Building**
- Job search self-efficacy is the #1 predictor of employment success — correlated with all three outcomes: number of offers, employment status, and unemployment duration (Kanfer, Wanberg & Kantrowitz, 2001; meta-analysis)
- Module: Systematic self-efficacy building through small wins, mastery experiences, and reframing
- Tool: Self-efficacy tracker (weekly confidence measurement + environmental audit)

**0.3 — Market Intelligence**
- Reading the labor market: how to interpret job posting trends, ghost job identification (18-27% of listings are ghost jobs — Greenhouse 2024), industry-specific hiring patterns
- LATAM-specific: remote work growth (161% in H1 2022), which industries are hiring, salary benchmarks by country
- Tool: Market scanning protocol — what to track, where, how often

**0.4 — Positioning Strategy**
- The strategic question: "What job should I pursue?" comes before "How do I get it?"
- Framework: Intersection of capability, market demand, and identity alignment
- Career change vs. career advancement — different strategies for each
- Person-Job fit (r=.56 with job satisfaction) vs. Person-Organization fit (r=.44) — know which matters more for your situation (Kristof-Brown et al., 2005; meta-analysis of 172 studies)

**Tool:** Full Compass Calibration — 42-question diagnostic across 7 axes (Propósito, Habilidades, Valores, Relaciones, Energía, Entorno, Recursos) with personalized report and action plan

#### STAGE 1: CV/Resume Optimization

**The problem this solves:** 97% of resumes are rejected. But 83% of recruiters say they're more likely to hire someone who tailored their resume. The gap is actionable.

**Content modules:**

**1.1 — How Hiring Actually Works**
- The 7.4-second scan: what recruiters look at and in what order (TheLadders eye-tracking)
- ATS myth debunked: 92% of ATS do NOT auto-reject (Enhancv 2025, n=25 recruiters). The real problem is volume — 250 applicants per posting, recruiter filters surface top 10-20
- Ghost jobs: 18-27% of postings have no real intent to hire (Greenhouse 2024). How to identify them before applying
- The application volume paradox: applications up 239% since ChatGPT, response rates at 3-5%

**1.2 — The Research-Based Resume System**
- Tailoring protocol: job description deconstruction → keyword mapping → experience matching (40-50% more callbacks — ResumeGo)
- Quantification system: how to convert any experience into measurable impact (only 26% of resumes have 5+ metrics — Cultivated Culture, n=125K)
- Optimal formatting: 475-600 words, tenure format for gaps (+15% — Kristal et al., Nature Human Behaviour), appropriate length by experience level
- Cover letter strategy: tailored cover letters produce 50% more interviews (ResumeGo) and 35.8% vs 21.2% offer rate. Template system for efficiency without losing personalization
- LATAM-specific norms: photo conventions by country, "hoja de vida" formatting, cedula/personal info conventions, education credential weighting

**1.3 — Digital Presence Optimization**
- LinkedIn: complete profiles get 40x more opportunities. Professional photo = 14x more views. Recommendations = 14x more views
- Social media audit: 70% of employers screen social media, 54% have rejected based on content (CareerBuilder)
- Portfolio/GitHub strategy for tech professionals: 75% of hiring managers consider portfolio a must-have

**1.4 — Bias Awareness**
- What you're up against and what you can control: racial callback gaps (36% fewer for Black candidates — Quillian et al., n=55,842), age discrimination (starts early 40s broadly, 29 in tech — Neumark et al., n=40,000+), name bias (50% more callbacks for white names — Bertrand & Mullainathan, n=5,000)
- "Resume whitening" reality: 2x more callbacks for minorities who scrub racial signals — but this happens even at "pro-diversity" companies (Kang et al., 2016)
- AI screening bias: LLMs favor white-associated names 85% of the time (UW 2024)
- What to do about it: formatting interventions, strategic emphasis, knowing when bias is the obstacle vs. resume quality

**Tool:** N1 Resume Builder Template (Notion) — pre-formatted for ATS compatibility, keyword tracking, quantification prompts, tailored cover letter generator workflow

#### STAGE 2: Screening Preparation

**The problem this solves:** 47% of candidates fail screens because they didn't research the company. 70% of hiring managers say being unprepared is the #1 mistake. These are preventable failures.

**Content modules:**

**2.1 — The Phone Screen Decoded**
- What recruiters actually evaluate (vs. what candidates prepare for): communication quality, self-awareness, enthusiasm calibration, question quality, collaboration signals
- The preparation-evaluation mismatch: candidates prepare technical skills; recruiters evaluate soft signals
- Semi-structured screen validity: r=.20-.30 — meaning the format itself has low predictive power, but preparation still dramatically changes outcomes

**2.2 — First Impression Science**
- First impressions form in 1/10th of a second, stabilize in 7-30 seconds (Decision Lab)
- Barrick et al. (2012): initial rapport-building impressions correlate r=.42 with final ratings — and r=.22 with actual job offers. First impressions predict hiring better than interview answers
- Structured response preparation to survive the snap judgment window

**2.3 — The STAR System (Advanced)**
- STAR method improves pass rates 30-50% (CareerBuilder)
- Behavioral questions are 55% effective at predicting performance vs. 10% for traditional questions (DDI)
- The N1 Story Bank: prepare 8-10 rich, detailed stories that can pivot across competency questions (conflict, failure, leadership, collaboration, ambiguity, prioritization, influence, innovation)
- Specificity training: names, numbers, timelines, outcomes. Not "we did X" but "I did X, measured by Y, resulting in Z"

**2.4 — Bias Navigation**
- Accent bias: d=0.47 medium effect, stronger in audio-only formats like phone screens (Spence et al. 2024, meta-analysis, n=4,576; Maindidze et al. 2025)
- Voice quality bias: 38% of impressions are voice-driven
- Video interview penalties: candidates receive lower ratings in video vs. in-person (Blacksmith et al. 2016 meta-analysis). Camera gaze effects — off-camera gaze scores worse than voice-only (Miura et al. 2024, Scientific Reports)
- For LATAM professionals interviewing with US/EU companies: accent mitigation, video setup optimization, cultural code-switching strategy

**2.5 — Ghosting Survival**
- 61% of candidates are ghosted post-interview (Greenhouse 2024, n=2,500). 75% of applications get zero response
- Psychological preparation: normalizing ghosting so it doesn't destroy self-efficacy
- Follow-up system: when, how, what to say, when to stop
- Ghost job detection: pre-application filters to avoid wasting effort

**Tool:** N1 Screen Prep Protocol (Notion template) — company research template, STAR story bank builder, question preparation framework, video setup checklist

#### STAGE 3: Technical Assessment Readiness

**The problem this solves:** ~80% of candidates fail technical interviews. Being observed reduces performance by >50% (Behroozi et al. 2020, RCT, n=48). The assessment measures assessment-taking ability, not job skill — creating a parallel skill economy.

**Content modules:**

**3.1 — Understanding the Assessment Landscape**
- Types: live coding, take-home, system design, pair programming, AI-proctored
- The fundamental disconnect: what's tested (algorithm implementation from memory) vs. what's done on the job (system design, debugging, collaboration)
- Practice volume benchmarks: 5+ mock interviews = ~2x pass rate improvement (interviewing.io), 20+ coding challenges = 50% higher pass rate (HackerRank)
- LeetCode ratings don't predict interview success (interviewing.io data) — quality over quantity

**3.2 — Performance Psychology**
- Stereotype threat: d=0.26 effect on performance for stereotyped groups (Steele & Aronson 1995)
- Impostor phenomenon: 52.7% of software engineers experience it (n=624, 26 countries)
- Choking under pressure: working memory overload mechanisms (Beilock & Carr, 2005)
- Women are 7x more likely to stop practicing after failure (interviewing.io platform data)
- Anxiety management protocols: breathing techniques, cognitive reframing, pre-performance routines

**3.3 — Structured Practice System**
- Practice schedule template calibrated to role level and timeline
- Mock interview protocol: how to practice effectively (not just repeatedly)
- The aggregation principle: multiple uncorrelated interview dimensions reduce aggregate error more than repeating the same type (Bernhardsson 2018)

**Tool:** N1 Technical Prep Tracker (Notion) — practice log, pattern recognition library, mock interview scheduling, anxiety management checklist

#### STAGE 4: Interview Mastery (Behavioral + Cultural)

**The problem this solves:** 58-73% of interviewed candidates are rejected. Structured interviews improve accuracy by 81% over unstructured (VidCruiter 2024) — but most companies use unstructured. Candidates must perform well in a broken system.

**Content modules:**

**4.1 — The Interview System Explained**
- Structured (r=.42-.51) vs. unstructured (r=.19-.38) — what the candidate faces and why it matters (Schmidt & Hunter 1998; Sackett et al. 2022; Wingate et al. 2025)
- Culture fit as social reproduction: evaluators use "culture fit" as a proxy for shared leisure activities, socioeconomic background, and personality similarity — not job performance (Rivera 2012, American Sociological Review)
- The extraversion bias: interviews reward extraversion (verbal fluency, rapid response, self-promotion) despite extraversion having r=.15 correlation with job performance — indistinguishable from zero (Barrick, Mount & Judge 2001)

**4.2 — Advanced STAR Execution**
- 8-10 stories covering: conflict, failure, leadership, collaboration, ambiguity, prioritization, influence, innovation
- Specificity protocol: each story has names, numbers, timelines, outcomes
- Pivot technique: how to adapt one story across multiple competency questions
- Self-awareness demonstration: discussing genuine failures with genuine lessons (not humble-brags)
- For introverts: leveraging written communication strengths in follow-up, requesting alternative formats when possible

**4.3 — Emotional Intelligence in Interviews**
- EI is trainable (Mattingly & Kraiger, 2019; meta-analysis on EI training)
- Practical EI in interviews: reading interviewer signals, calibrating enthusiasm (authentic engagement, not performative), active listening, conversational reciprocity
- Negativity avoidance: framing all past experiences constructively without blame

**4.4 — Panel and Multi-Round Preparation**
- Panel interviews have inter-rater reliability of .74 vs. .44 for individual interviews
- Multi-round strategy: consistency across interviewers, energy management, information building across rounds
- Debrief self-protocol after each round

**Tool:** N1 Interview Command Center (Notion) — story bank with competency tags, interviewer research template, round-by-round tracker, self-debrief protocol

#### STAGE 5: Negotiation & Offer Management

**The problem this solves:** 55% of candidates never negotiate despite 89% of employers being open to it. Those who negotiate receive 18.83% more on average. The gap is enormous and addressable.

**Content modules:**

**5.1 — Negotiation Fundamentals**
- The core data: 18.83% average gain for negotiators (Interview Guys 2024-2025 meta-review). 66% get what they ask for (Pew Research 2023). 89% of employers are open to it (XpertHR 2021)
- The gender gap: women are penalized for negotiating the same way men do (Bowles, Babcock & Lai, 2007). "Relational" framing reduces the social penalty
- Racial gap: Black candidates face backlash for the same negotiation behaviors (Hernandez et al. 2019)
- For LATAM: salary benchmarks by country, PPP considerations when negotiating with US/EU companies, currency and tax implications of remote work

**5.2 — Research-Based Negotiation Tactics**
- Use precise numbers: $87,250 not $85,000 — signals preparation and produces more conciliatory counter-offers (Mason et al. 2013, Journal of Experimental Social Psychology)
- Come with data: employees with specific salary data are 40% more likely to achieve goals (PayScale 2021)
- Frame around value, not need: "Based on my skills in managing X, $Y reflects the value I bring" (Harvard PON)
- Negotiate total compensation: signing bonuses, equity, remote work, PTO, professional development are all levers
- Get it in writing: email negotiation creates paper trail and reduces social penalty (especially important for women and minorities)
- Collaborative vs. competitive framing: both work, gaining ~$5,000 more on average

**5.3 — Counter-Offer Analysis**
- 90% of people who accept counter-offers leave within 12 months
- Framework for evaluating whether a counter-offer addresses the underlying dissatisfaction or just the financial symptom
- Decision matrix template

**5.4 — Offer Evaluation Framework**
- Beyond salary: total compensation modeling, role trajectory, company trajectory, cultural signals, growth potential
- Red flag identification at the offer stage
- When NOT to negotiate: explicitly non-negotiable offers, relationship risk assessment, market position evaluation

**Tool:** N1 Negotiation Playbook (Notion) — salary research template, precise number calculator, total compensation comparison matrix, counter-offer decision framework, email negotiation scripts

## 4. Methodology: "The N1 System"

### Name

**El Sistema N1** — "Estrategia de carrera basada en ciencia"

### Tagline

`psicología x sistemas x aceleración`

### Core Philosophy

Career change is an identity transition, not a skills problem. The system fails on both sides (hiring processes are broken) but individual strategy still determines outcomes. The N1 System is the first methodology that:
1. Starts with psychology (who you are becoming), not tactics (how to write a resume)
2. Uses structured systems at every stage (because structure improves outcomes by 30-81% depending on the stage)
3. Backs every recommendation with a named study (because credibility is built on evidence, not opinion)
4. Accelerates through execution velocity (because knowledge without action is the default failure mode — 12.6% course completion says it all)

### The Four Pillars

#### Pillar 1: Psicología (Psychology)

The internal transformation that makes external change possible.

| Component | Research Foundation |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Identity transition | Career change follows identity transition patterns (Annual Reviews of Org Psych, 2024) |
| Self-efficacy building | #1 predictor of job search success — correlated with all 3 outcomes (Kanfer et al. 2001, meta-analysis) |
| Grief processing | Job loss triggers grief response — denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance |
| Impostor syndrome management | 52.7% of software engineers experience it (n=624, 26 countries) |
| Stereotype threat awareness | d=0.26 performance impact for stereotyped groups (Steele & Aronson 1995) |
| Bias resilience | Knowing the system is biased without internalizing rejection as personal failure |

#### Pillar 2: Sistemas (Systems)

Structured processes that outperform intuition at every stage.

| Component | Research Foundation |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Structured preparation | STAR method improves pass rates 30-50% (CareerBuilder). Structured interviews improve accuracy 81% (VidCruiter) |
| Tracking and measurement | Search quality > search quantity (Van Hooft et al. 2021). Self-regulation processes matter more than raw effort |
| Feedback loops | 5-phase cycle: Identificación → Diagnóstico → Trabajo → Retroalimentación → Reinicio |
| Application targeting | Only 20% of roles are filled through postings. Referrals produce the best hires (Zottoli & Wanous 2000). Stop mass-applying. |
| Market intelligence | Ghost job filtering (18-27% of postings), industry-specific response rates, volume optimization |

#### Pillar 3: Ciencia (Science)

Every recommendation backed by a named study.

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Cite the source | Every claim includes author, year, sample size, and finding |
| Prefer meta-analyses | Aggregate findings over individual studies — larger samples, more reliable |
| Acknowledge confidence levels | Strong (meta-analyses, large RCTs), Moderate (field studies, smaller samples), Weak (surveys, self-reported) |
| Update with new research | Monthly literature scan for new findings that update or contradict existing recommendations |
| Distinguish correlation from causation | Never claim "X causes Y" when the evidence shows "X correlates with Y" |

#### Pillar 4: Aceleración (Acceleration)

Execution velocity as a core competency.

| Component | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| Action bias over analysis paralysis | Weekly action commitments with community accountability |
| Sprint cycles | 4-week focused sprints on one stage at a time |
| Peer accountability | Weekly check-ins, buddy system, community visibility |
| Minimum viable applications | Don't perfect endlessly — deploy, measure, iterate |
| Time-boxing | 2-hour blocks for deep work (resume tailoring, interview prep) inspired by The 2-Hour Job Search methodology |

### The N1 Cycle

The methodology operates as a cycle, not a linear funnel:

```
Identificación → Diagnóstico → Trabajo → Retroalimentación → Reinicio
  (Who am I?)    (Where am I?)  (Execute)   (What happened?)   (Adjust)
```

Each stage of the hiring funnel (0-6) passes through this cycle. For example:
- **Resume stage:** Identify your positioning → Diagnose the gap between your resume and the role → Do the tailoring work → Track callbacks and response rates → Adjust approach based on data
- **Interview stage:** Identify your story themes → Diagnose preparation gaps → Practice with mocks → Debrief each interview → Refine stories and approach

## 7. Unique Moats

### 7.1 Scientific Sourcing

**What it is:** Every recommendation in N1 cites a named study with author, year, sample size, and finding. Not "studies show" — "Quillian et al. (2017), meta-analysis of 28 field experiments (n=55,842), found that whites receive 36% more callbacks than African Americans, with no improvement since 1989."

**Why it's a moat:** No competitor in any language does this consistently. Books cite studies but don't build methodology around them. Courses reference data but don't give sources. Coaching uses frameworks without evidence bases. N1's research library (4,794 lines across 8 files, 200+ sources, 30+ meta-analyses) is the foundation for every piece of content and every tool.

**Defensibility:** Continuous research updates. Monthly literature scans for new meta-analyses and field experiments. The research advantage compounds over time.

### 7.2 Spanish-First

**What it is:** Built in Spanish, for Spanish-speaking professionals, with LATAM cultural context. Not translated from English.

**Why it's a moat:**
- No subscription-based career change methodology exists in Spanish at any scale
- No career community at scale serves LATAM professionals
- Translated US products don't account for: voseo/tuteo regional norms, country-specific labor markets, LATAM resume conventions (photo, cedula, hoja de vida), coaching pricing ($8-35/session in Colombia vs. $100-500/hr in US), different professional norms, informal economy dynamics
- Cultural authenticity in voice, examples, and context creates organic sharing and trust

**Defensibility:** First-mover advantage in a massive underserved market. Language + culture moat is expensive for US competitors to cross. Network effects in a Spanish-language community compound.

### 7.3 Psychology Integration

**What it is:** Career change treated as an identity transition, not a skills problem. Self-efficacy, grief processing, impostor syndrome, stereotype threat, and bias resilience are core to the methodology — not add-ons.

**Why it's a moat:**
- 80% of job seekers feel unprepared (LinkedIn 2026) — the problem is psychological, not informational
- Self-efficacy is the #1 predictor of job search success (Kanfer et al. 2001) — yet no career product systematically builds it
- Every AI tool, resume builder, and interview course ignores the internal transformation that makes external change possible
- Psychology is the one dimension AI cannot replicate

**Defensibility:** Requires genuine expertise in both career strategy and psychology. Can't be copied by adding a "mindset module" to a resume tool.

### 7.4 Community Accountability

**What it is:** Live weekly calls, peer matching, accountability structures, cohort intensives, community-driven referral network.

**Why it's a moat:**
- Async courses fail: 12.6% median completion rate (MOOC research)
- Community solves the accountability gap that books, courses, and tools don't
- Peer relationships create switching costs — people stay for the community even after achieving their career goal
- As community grows, it becomes a referral network — and referrals produce the best hires (Zottoli & Wanous, 2000)

**Defensibility:** Community compounds. A 100-person engaged community with shared methodology, peer relationships, and referral connections is nearly impossible to replicate.

### 7.5 System-Level Thinking

**What it is:** N1 integrates the full arc from "I need to change" through "I'm thriving in my new role" into one system. Not a resume tool. Not an interview course. Not a coaching session. A complete methodology.

**Why it's a moat:**
- Every existing product solves one piece of the puzzle. Teal fixes resumes ($4M users). Interviewing.io practices interviews. Books provide knowledge. Coaches provide guidance. None integrates psychology + strategy + tools + community + accountability into a single system
- The hiring funnel has interdependencies: identity clarity (Stage 0) determines positioning (Stage 1) which determines screening performance (Stage 2) which determines interview quality (Stage 4) which determines negotiation leverage (Stage 5). Solving one stage without the others produces diminishing returns
- AI commoditizes individual tactics (resume writing, interview prep). Strategy is the differentiator. N1 owns the strategy layer

**Defensibility:** Integration is hard. Building a coherent system across 7 stages, 4 pillars, 15 tools, and a live community is operationally intensive. Competitors would need to rebuild the entire stack.
